Understanding BMI Categories

Category BMI range What it means
UnderweightBelow 18.5May indicate malnutrition or an underlying condition.
Normal weight18.5 – 24.9Considered a healthy weight for most adults.
Overweight25 – 29.9Slightly elevated risk of some health conditions.
Obese30 and aboveHigher risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and more.

BMI is a quick screening tool, not a diagnosis. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition. Athletes, pregnant women, and children need different assessments — always consult a healthcare professional for a full picture.

How BMI is Calculated

BMI (Body Mass Index) is calculated using a simple formula:

Metric: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)²
Imperial: BMI = (weight (lb) × 703) / height (in)²

For example, someone who weighs 70 kg and is 1.75 m tall has a BMI of 70 / (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9 — a normal weight.
