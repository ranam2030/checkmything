How to Use the Age Calculator

  1. 1. Enter your date of birth in the first field. You can type it or pick it from the date picker.
  2. 2. Check the second date. It's set to today by default, but you can change it to any date you like — past or future — to calculate age as of that specific day.
  3. 3. Click Calculate. Your exact age will appear in years, months, and days, along with total days, weeks, and hours lived.
  4. 4. Read your personalised health tips based on the life stage that matches your calculated age.

This calculator is ideal for finding how old someone was on a historical date, checking eligibility for age-based programmes, calculating a child's age for school admission, or working out how long until a future milestone.

Why Accurate Age Calculation Matters

Age is more than a number on a birthday card — it's a key input for medical decisions, legal processes, retirement planning, insurance quotes, school admissions, and visa applications. A simple mental shortcut like "subtract the years" often misses months and days, which can matter when eligibility cut-offs are strict.

Our calculator handles the details for you: leap years, varying month lengths, and February 29 birthdays. The date-to-date mode lets you answer questions like "How old was I on my wedding day?" or "How old will I be when my child graduates?" in one click.

Health Tips by Age and Life Stage

Wellness needs change with every decade. Here's a quick overview — personalised tips appear above once you calculate your age.

Infants & Toddlers (0–2)

Focus on breastfeeding or formula, timely vaccinations, 12–16 hours of sleep, and rich early language exposure for brain development.

Children (3–12)

60+ minutes of daily play, balanced meals with fruit and whole grains, 9–12 hours of sleep, and limited screen time build lifelong habits.

Teens (13–19)

Enough protein, calcium, and iron for growth, regular activity, 8–10 hours of sleep, and open conversations about mental health.

Young Adults (20–35)

150 minutes of moderate activity weekly, strength training, whole-food meals, and 7–9 hours of consistent sleep lock in habits that compound for decades.

Adults (36–50)

Annual blood pressure, cholesterol, and glucose checks. Keep strength training to protect metabolism and bone density. Manage stress actively.

Middle-Aged (51–65)

Age-appropriate screenings, balance and flexibility work, protein- and fibre-rich meals, and ongoing learning for cognitive resilience.

Seniors (66+)

Daily gentle movement like walking or tai chi, regular check-ups, fall-prevention at home, and strong social connection — loneliness affects health as much as smoking.

These are general wellness tips, not medical advice. Please consult a qualified clinician for anything specific to you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the age calculator work? ▾

It takes two dates — your date of birth and a target date — and computes the exact difference in years, months, and days, accounting for leap years and variable month lengths.

Can I calculate age between two custom dates? ▾

Yes. The second field defaults to today but you can change it to any date, past or future. This is useful for finding how old someone was on a specific historical date.

Is this calculator free? ▾

Completely free, no sign-up, and no usage limits.

Is my date of birth stored anywhere? ▾

No. All calculations run in your browser — nothing is sent to a server or saved.

How accurate is it? ▾

Accurate to the day. We handle leap years, month-length differences, and February 29 edge cases.
