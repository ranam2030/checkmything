How the Due Date is Estimated

This calculator uses Naegele's rule, the standard clinical method: it adds 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of your last menstrual period. This assumes a regular 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14.

Only about 4% of babies are born exactly on their estimated due date — most arrive within a window of 2 weeks before to 2 weeks after. Your doctor may adjust the date based on ultrasound measurements.

Trimesters at a Glance

First Trimester
Weeks 1–12. Major organ development; most common time for morning sickness.
Second Trimester
Weeks 13–26. Often called the "golden" trimester — energy usually improves.
Third Trimester
Weeks 27–40. Final growth and preparation for birth.

This tool provides general information and is not a substitute for medical care. Always consult your doctor or midwife for personal pregnancy guidance.
